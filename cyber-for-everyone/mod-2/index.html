Metadata

Now let's talk about a topic that is very interesting and important; technically we call them METADATA, but don't worry the concept is extremely simple: metadata is: data about data, just as it sounds; let's illustrate it with an example: if we take any book, on its cover we will see the title of the book and maybe its author (that information would be the equivalent of the file name), but if we want to know what chapters it contains, date of publication, edition, etc. we would have to take the book, open it and look for that data inside the book. That is the equivalent to the METADATA; not the content of the book itself but is information related to the book contained in the same book.

The issue we are interested in, is the data contained within a file, which we normally do not see, but which become very useful to an attacker; for example, what device a photo was taken with, its geolocation at the time it was taken, the author or creator of the file, the Operating System of the device on which the file was created or last edited, etc.

Having said the above, I want you to think about all the files, images, videos, that you have sent by mail, uploaded to web pages, or shared by any other means; how much of your information did you deliver along with the files?

We can't do anything with the past, but we can with the present and the future, so if you want to know what data your files have inside, let’s find out!

Metadata in Documents

Metadata in a document refers to information about the document itself, such as its author, date of creation, file size, format, and keywords. It can also include information about the document's content, such as the title, subject, and summary.

Metadata is often hidden from the user and can be accessed and viewed by software applications that can read and interpret it. This information can be useful for organizing and searching for documents, as well as for understanding the context and history of the document.

For example, if you create a Word document, the metadata might include the document title, author name, date of creation, and keywords that describe the content. This information can help you locate the document later by searching for specific keywords or by sorting the files by date or author.

In summary, metadata is information that provides context and details about a document and its content, and it can be used to help organize, search, and manage documents.

Cellphone Number Search

Is very common to receive unsolicited, potentially malicious calls or SMS ; and is important to know how to search the true origin of the communication.

There could be several reasons why you may need to search for an unknown cell phone number. Here are a few possible scenarios:

  1. Missed call or text message: If you receive a call or text message from an unknown number, you may want to find out who it is so that you can decide whether to respond or not.
  1. Harassing or spam calls: If you are receiving repeated calls from an unknown number and suspect that it might be a spam or harassing call, you may want to identify the caller so that you can take appropriate action.
  1. Business purposes: If you are in business, you may need to search for the contact details of potential clients or partners, and sometimes, all you may have is their phone number.
  1. Reconnecting with someone: If you have lost touch with someone and only have their phone number, you may want to find out if it is still active and if they are reachable.
  1. Safety concerns: If you have a concern for your safety, you may want to identify the person behind an unknown number before you decide whether to answer their call or not.

Privacy Oriented Internet Browsers

Privacy browsers, are web browsers designed to protect users' online privacy and security. These browsers incorporate various features and technologies to help users surf the internet with greater privacy and security.

Some of the key features of privacy browsers include:

  1. Ad blocking - Privacy browsers can block ads and trackers, preventing them from collecting users' data and following their online activities.
  1. Fingerprinting protection - Privacy browsers can prevent websites from collecting unique information about users' devices, such as browser type, fonts, screen size, and more.
  1. HTTPS upgrades - Privacy browsers can automatically upgrade insecure HTTP connections to secure HTTPS connections, ensuring that users' data is encrypted and protected.
  1. Private browsing mode - Privacy browsers offer a private browsing mode that doesn't store users' browsing history, cookies, or other data.
  1. Tor integration - Some privacy browsers offer integration with the Tor network, which can help users further anonymize their online activities.

Overall, privacy browsers are designed to give users greater control over their online privacy and security, helping to protect them from unwanted tracking, surveillance, and data collection.

How do I browse safely?

If you don't want to use a Privacy Oriented Browser, you can install some tools (Add-Ons) in your regular browser (Firefox, Chrome, etc) to increase you Security and privacy; Some of the recommended tools are:

Addon: Netcraft

This extension compatible with Firefox, opera and chrome helps us to detect sites with bad reputation or that are impersonating a legitimate site (Phishing).

Addon: Ghostery

Ghostery is a solution that helps us to detect and block ads, tags and trackers. It can be installed as a Firefox and chrome browser add-on for computers (Linux, Mac OSX and Windows).

On mobile devices (Android, iOS) Ghostery can be installed as a browser.

Addon: Adblock Plus

It is a blocker of pop-up windows and advertising, this add-on is available for all major browsers (Chrome, Firefox, Opera, Safari and Internet Explorer), it is also compatible with Android (Adblock browser).

Addon: No Script

No Script is an extension for Firefox and Firefox based browsers such as monkey, among others. This extension allows us to restrict the execution of code (java, JavaScript, flash) and plugins on websites.

Addon: Ublock Origin

Ublock Origin that allows us to filter content and advertising on the Internet, this extension is compatible with Firefox, Safari, Chrome, Chromium, Opera and Microsoft edge browsers.

Addon: Https Everywhere

It is an extension compatible with all major browsers that requires the use of encrypted connections when browsing websites.

VPN (Virtual Private Network)

If you want even more anonymity and protection about your communications, you can always use a VPN; but first: What is a VPN?

It's very simple; let's think of our internet connection as a highway; then we build a tunnel where cars, trucks, etc. are going to travel over that same highway; if someone looks at the tunnel from the outside, they only see the construction, but have no idea how many cars, trucks or what kind of vehicles are traveling through the tunnel; that's what a VPN does; it creates an encrypted channel within your normal connection so you have a higher level of privacy.

In other words, a VPN creates a secure tunnel between your device and the internet, which keeps your online activity hidden and your personal information safe. It also allows you to access the internet as if you were in a different location, which can be helpful if you need to access content that is restricted in your country.

There are many VPN services, some free, others paid; the recommendation is that if you are interested in a VPN you should look for an affordable but paid VPN; remember that when a product is free.... YOU are the product, in other words, NOTHING is really free.

If you want to select a VPN service, you can take into account factors such as privacy, anonymity, price, features, etc.
