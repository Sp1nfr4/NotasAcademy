How do I protect my devices?

Protecting your devices against cybersecurity threats is crucial to keep your personal and sensitive information secure. Here are some tips to help you protect your devices:

Keep your software up to date:

Make sure that you install updates for your operating system, software, and apps regularly.

These updates often contain security patches that fix vulnerabilities that can be exploited by malicious hackers or Cybercriminals.

Use strong passwords

Use complex passwords that are difficult for malicious hackers to guess.

Avoid using the same password across multiple accounts, and use a password managers to keep track of your passwords in only ona place easy to access.

Remember: To change the last character or put a symbol as last character dont make the pasword "safe".

Enable two-factor authentication

Two-factor authentication (2FA), adds an extra layer of security to your accounts by requiring a second form of verification, such as a fingerprint or a code sent to your phone.

Some examples can be:

  • Use a Password + Code received by SMS
  • Use a Password + Code received by Email
  • Use a Password + FaceID

Some Multifactor Authentication tools:

  • Microsoft Authenticator
  • Google Authenticator
  • Yubico

Be cautious of suspicious emails

Don't open emails from unknown senders, and be wary of attachments and links in emails that look suspicious.

These may contain malware that can infect your device and compromise your information.

Some suspicious contents:

  • Spelling and Grammatical errors
  • Ambiguous Content
  • Senders that you never met
  • Unsolicited Requests
  • Be wary of urgent requests
