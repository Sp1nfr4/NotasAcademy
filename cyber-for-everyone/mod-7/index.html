Secure use of Clouds

Day by day we put more and more information in services that are given to us or offered to us at a very good price, to store our most precious information: Photos, Videos, Music, Documents, etc., as if they were an additional Hard Disk, but to which we connect through the Internet.

The problem with the growing use of cloud storage, is that no one has told us how to use them safely, or at least tell us how to protect the data I stored in my cloud. It's very important to ensure the security of your data while using the cloud. Here are some key considerations for secure use of the cloud:

Choose a reputable cloud provider

The first step in secure use of the cloud is to choose a cloud provider that has a good reputation for security. Research providers thoroughly before choosing one to ensure that they have strong security protocols in place. The most known Cloud providers are:

  • OneDrive
  • Dropbox
  • Google Drive

Use strong passwords and two-factor authentication

I Know we have said this enough, however, we have to let you know how important is to enable two-factor authentication and the use of strong and unique passwords for all cloud accounts. When you enable two-factor authentication this will ensure that even if your password is compromised, your data remains secure.

Two -Factor Authentication

Encrypt your Data

Encrypting your data is an essential step in securing it in the cloud. Use strong encryption algorithms to ensure that your data is protected from unauthorized access. Here are some tools that encrypt data in Cloud Storage:

  • VeraCrypt
  • Cryptomator
  • NordLocker

Be careful with file sharing

When sharing files in the cloud, be careful to only share them with trusted parties. Consider setting permissions for files so that only authorized users can access them.

Monitor your account activity

Regularly monitor your cloud account activity to ensure that no unauthorized access has occurred. If you notice any suspicious activity, take action immediately to protect your data.

Keep your software up to date

You must be asking yourself "Again! really?" but is very important to make sure you don't skip this step! Make sure that all software used to access the cloud is up to date with the latest security patches. Outdated software can create vulnerabilities that malicious hackers can exploit.

Backup your data

Always keep a backup of your data in case of any data loss or other issues. Make sure that backups are stored securely and are easily accessible in case of emergency.
